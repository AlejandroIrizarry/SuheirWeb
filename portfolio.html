Project #3: Casa Flores - Functional Diversity Home

Image description
Image description
Image description
Image description

Casa Flores is a sustainable concept that proposes the redesign of a predetermined home. These integrate elements that respond to a universal design of adaptable spaces, tropical and resilient environments. It enriches the family comfort by keeping a central module with open systems that ventilate and illuminate a large part of the residence. The structure has a prefabricated system of polystyrene, steel and cement cladding which makes it more resistant to earthquakes, hurricanes and fires. Its geometry also allows easy construction thanks to its modular uniformity between spaces. The house is a 2,250 square foot modular house consisting of a central module that contains the basic services and two lateral modules that have public and private areas. The conceptual idea arises from a furniture in which the movement of two volumes and a fixed center are observed, anchoring the reference of the seed module. Three elements and design principles are subtracted from it in order to develop the interior and exterior space. Formalizing the vertical and horizontal lines that intersect helping to determine the configuration of areas. In addition, overlaps of rectangles and squares that suggest directionality that give a feeling of welcoming. On the other hand, the presence of openings and solids are also obtained as a third element. Allowing the residence to be not only an aesthetically dynamic configuration, but also contains a comfortable and pleasant space for the components of the family.
